My first term at uni!

A short glimpse of my time and experience at Imperial

  • Next

Settling in

My assigned halls of residence is Wilson House, in the Paddington area. Having not lived in London before, I really didn't know what to expect. Luckily for me, it's a beautiful place. Clean and well-maintained, with every basic amenity a uni student would need in under a 10 minute walk. I was fortunate to be put in a corner room, with two windows providing plenty of sunlight.

Exploring Hyde Park

Living pretty much on the doorstep of Hyde Park obviously meant that I had to take a look through it the first day. Needless to say, it is absolutely incredible - I really didn't expect it to be so big or to be filled with so much.

Welcome/Induction Week

The first couple weeks of term were jam-packed with activities. I had the chance to meet loads of new people through all events organised by my hall, as well as the Union. Hockey freshers' trials was another highlight for me.

Social Events

Literally every day of the first fortnight had an event on, to the point where it was difficult to choose which ones to attend! Unfortunately, I only footage I took was me getting back at around 2am after Show Your Colours.

Induction Project

Right from the get-go, our department decided to have us making things, with our first group task being to build a Rube Goldberg machine to light up a small LED. Depsite having not done Design Technology in the past, I found it quite enjoyable to build something physical so early on.

Exploring London

Of course, I couldn't just confine myself to Paddington. Together with a few friends, I visited some other areas of London to see just what kind of views the city had to offer.

Cooking!

At uni, one of the biggest changes to my day-to-day life turned out to be the amount of cooking that I'd have to do. I considered myself average in terms of culinary skill, so saw starting uni as a good time to improve on my techniques and prove to doubters (mostly my friends) that I could, in fact, make some good food.

Hockey at Imperial

As I mentioned, hockey trials were during our welcome week. I was quite nervous, as I didn't know how good the standard of hockey would be at the uni. Fortunately, I played quite well at the trials, which led to me being picked for the first team. We've had several matches in the first term, both home and away against the likes of Exeter, Cardiff, Cambridge, etc. You can also see my first goal scored from a dragflick below (warning: loud).

All in all...

It has been and amazing first term at Imperial. Sure the workload is quite tough, but I have to say that I am thoroughly enjoying it. Admittedly, I was at some point feeling some trepidation surrounding whether I had made the right choice, but now I realise that my fears were unfounded.

Looking forward to a great term 2!
